Thiruvananthapuram,

also known as Trivandrum, is famous for its ancient temples, cultural heritage, beaches, and Ayurvedic treatments. It is the capital city of Kerala and boasts a rich history and numerous attractions.
Here's a more detailed look at what makes Thiruvananthapuram famous:
Historical and Cultural Significance:
Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple:
This ancient temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu is one of the richest religious institutions in the world, according to Travelsetu.com.
Ancient Temples:
The city is home to numerous other temples, showcasing a blend of Dravidian and colonial architectural styles.
Cultural Heritage:
Thiruvananthapuram is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, including traditional art forms and festivals.
Historical Monuments:
The city features several historical monuments, including palaces and forts, reflecting its royal past.
Museums and Art Galleries:
The city has notable museums like the Napier Museum and the Sree Chitra Art Gallery, displaying a range of artifacts and artworks.
Chalai Bazaar:
This traditional market in the Fort area offers a glimpse into the city's vibrant commercial life.
Kowdiar Palace:
A former royal residence, now a historical landmark, attracting visitors for its architectural grandeur.
Natural Beauty and Wellness:
Beaches: Kovalam beach, known for its scenic beauty and lighthouse, is a popular tourist destination.
Backwaters: The city's backwaters offer serene boat rides and picturesque landscapes.
Ayurvedic Treatments: Thiruvananthapuram is a hub for Ayurvedic treatments and wellness centers, attracting those seeking holistic healing and rejuvenation.
Other Notable Aspects:
Academic Hub:
The city is a major center for education and research, with several renowned institutions.
Manufacturing and IT Industries:
Thiruvananthapuram has a growing economy, with manufacturing and information technology being key sectors, according to Wikipedia.
Capital City:
As the capital of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram plays a significant role in the state's administration, politics, and culture.
