Kottayam,

often called the "Land of Letters" or "Akshara Nagari," is renowned for its high literacy rate, extensive rubber plantations, and literary heritage. It's also known for its backwaters, particularly Kumarakom, and its significant contribution to India's rubber production. Here's a more detailed look at what makes Kottayam famous:

1. Literacy: Kottayam is recognized as the first town in India to achieve 100% literacy, earning it the nickname "Akshara Nagari" (City of Letters).
2. Rubber Plantations: The district is a major hub for rubber cultivation in India, with vast plantations contributing significantly to the country's rubber production, according to the Kottayam District website.
3. Literary Heritage: Kottayam has a strong literary tradition, with a thriving print media and publishing industry.
4. Backwaters and Kumarakom: The Vembanad Lake and Kumarakom backwaters are popular tourist destinations, offering scenic beauty, houseboat cruises, and the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary.
5. Tourist Attractions: Besides the backwaters, Kottayam boasts other attractions like:
Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple: An ancient temple known for its intricate architecture and religious significance.
Vaikom Mahadeva Temple: One of the oldest Shiva temples in Kerala, famous for its annual festival.
Nadukani hills: Offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
Marmala Waterfall: A scenic waterfall surrounded by adventurous roads.
Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary: A haven for birdwatchers with diverse avian species.
Poonjar Palace: A historical palace showcasing traditional Kerala architecture.
6. Cultural Arts: Kottayam is known for its folk dances like Margamkali and Arjuna Nritham, popular among the Syrian Christian community.
7. Food: Kottayam is also known for its local cuisine, including the popular snack Kottayam Churuttu, a sweet and crispy treat.
